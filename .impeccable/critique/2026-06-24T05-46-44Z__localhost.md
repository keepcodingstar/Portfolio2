## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated? No — it's genuinely distinctive.** The altitude journey (space↑/sky/ground↓), the WebGL cloud curtain, the black-hole apex, the HUD/mono "altimeter" system, and the custom Awesome Serif give it a real POV. A visitor asks "how was this made?", not "which AI made this?" — it passes the brand slop test.

**Aesthetic-lane caution (second-order).** The *work zone* — ruled rows + right-aligned tabular numerals + small mono labels + italic display serif — is almost exactly the "editorial-typographic" reflex lane (display-serif-italic + mono labels + rules + monochrome restraint) that has saturated Stripe/Notion-adjacent brand surfaces. The surrounding spatial concept lifts it above the lane, but the work zone is the one place the design momentarily follows the crowd.

**Repeated kicker.** A tiny uppercase mono tracked eyebrow sits above space ("THE CREATIVE SIDE · OUT PAST THE FUNNEL"), work ("SELECTED WORK"), and ground ("ON THE GROUND · ROOTS & CONTACT"). Repeated section-kickers are a known AI-grammar tell. Here they're partly redeemed — the copy carries the altitude narrative and the mono face is a deliberate HUD system — so it's voice-leaning, but it's at the edge.

**Deterministic scan.** The bundled detector over `app/` + `components/` returned **one** finding: a side-stripe `border-left: 3px solid var(--ion)` at `app/work/work.css:492`, used on the `/work/checkout` and `/work/fair-pricing` case pages. That's an absolute ban (the "AI stripe"), but it is **not** on the home journey. The home zone components scanned clean.

## Overall Impression
The concept is the strength and it's a real one — this is a portfolio that argues for the designer by being well-designed. The single biggest opportunity: the cloud layer, which is the site's signature flourish, is currently fighting the two pieces of content that matter most — the hero value-prop and the work metrics (the proof). Get the clouds to *frame* rather than *cover* those, and the score jumps.

## What's Working
1. **A committed POV, not a template.** Per-section art direction (space floats off-grid; work is ruled and measured) is exactly the brand-register permission used well — the contrast is intentional and legible as meaning.
2. **A coherent, committed palette + type system.** The cool ion/aurora axis is held consistently (no warm-default drift), the custom serif + mono HUD labels are distinctive, and the new grassland footer pays the journey off with a satisfying landing.
3. **Motion done responsibly.** Ambitious first-load choreography (the cloud curtain), yet every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` resolve via `gsap.matchMedia`. Brand-grade ambition without the accessibility tax.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] The cloud layer obscures the hero headline.** On the sky (the landing screen, the most important fold), "Design for outcomes, numbers that move." competes with cloud cover *and* the portrait behind it; in capture it's barely legible. *Caveat: headless WebGL (swiftshader) + curtain timing may overstate this — verify live — but the risk is real.* This directly violates the project's own principle #3 ("immersion in service of reading"). **Fix:** ensure the resting cloud bank clears the centre column completely, and/or add a soft radial scrim behind the headline so it always reads regardless of cloud position. **Suggested command:** /impeccable polish
- **[P1] Clouds collide with the work-zone case content.** The `WORK_EDGE` clouds overlap the metrics and case titles; the conversion-critical proof ("500%", "+2.68%", "Checkout migration") is partly buried, and the ion-cyan numerals sit on a bright pale-blue with weak contrast. This is the surface the whole site is built to deliver a reader to. **Fix:** pull the work clouds fully to the margins (or drop them here), and darken/contrast the metric numerals against the work background. **Suggested command:** /impeccable layout
- **[P2] Work-zone leans into the saturated editorial-typographic lane.** Ruled rows + tabular numerals + mono labels + italic serif is the saturated fingerprint. **Fix:** differentiate the work zone's treatment (e.g. lean harder into the "instrument readout / altimeter" metaphor that's unique to this site, rather than the generic ruled-ledger), or vary the kicker cadence so it isn't an eyebrow-per-section. **Suggested command:** /impeccable typeset
- **[P2] Orientation cost: HUD labels + inverted scroll.** First-timers must learn that scroll-up reveals *more* content and decode abbreviated nav labels (VOID/SPACE/SKY/WORK/GROUND). The dual scroll cues help, but the value prop must survive the learning moment. **Fix:** keep the HUD labels but pair them with the plain word on hover/active, and make the up-scroll affordance unmissable on first load. **Suggested command:** /impeccable clarify
- **[P3] Metric contrast independent of clouds.** Even with clouds removed, large ion (`#2bb6d6`) numerals on the pale-blue work background likely miss the 3:1 large-text floor. **Suggested command:** /impeccable colorize

## Persona Red Flags

**Priya — Design Director (project persona, from PRODUCT.md: time-poor hiring lead, the primary audience).** She lands on a hero she can't fully read (clouds over the value prop), scrolls to the proof, and finds the metrics — her actual decision input — partly obscured by the same clouds. She's evaluating on a clock; the payload she needs (a case study) sits behind a legibility tax. **Risk: she bounces before reaching a case study — which is the site's stated success metric.**

**Jordan — Confused First-Timer.** The scroll-up-for-creative convention is non-standard and unexplained until she notices the small cue; the obscured headline means the 5-second "what is this?" test is shaky; "VOID" as a nav label is cryptic.

**Casey — Distracted Mobile User.** *Not yet verified — flag.* A full WebGL cloud field + black-hole shader is a load/perf concern on 3G and mid phones, and the large `clamp()` display headings ("numbers that move") risk overflow on narrow widths. Needs a mobile capture pass.

## Minor Observations
- The space-zone eyebrow, orbit experiments, and lede all read cleanly — that zone is the strongest realized fold.
- Side-nav glass rail renders well over both the dark space and the bright sky; good cross-section robustness.
- The grassland footer's colophon contrast was just corrected (light-on-grass) and now reads.
- `border-left` stripe in `work.css:492` should be rewritten (full border / tint / leading numeral) on the case pages.

## Questions to Consider
- If the hero headline is the entire pitch, should *anything* ever cover it — or should the clouds be choreographed to rest fully clear of the centre, always?
- The work zone is your proof. Should the clouds touch it at all?
- Is the ruled-row "ledger" treatment in the work zone the one place the design follows the crowd — and would the unique "instrument readout" metaphor serve it better?
