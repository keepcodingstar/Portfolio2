# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, design leads, and design directors at product companies,
evaluating Sameer Kapil for a senior / lead product-design role. They arrive
time-poor and skeptical — often mid-way through a stack of portfolios — and
decide in seconds whether to keep reading. Their job-to-be-done is two-staged:
first, judge quickly whether Sameer is worth a conversation; then, if convinced,
go deep on a single case study to assess how he actually thinks. Success for the
visitor is finishing one case study with a clear sense of his rigor and range.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Sameer Kapil — a Bangalore-based product designer who
"designs for outcomes, not applause": checkout, pricing, and trust flows that
move real numbers (500K+ users reached). The site exists to turn a busy,
skeptical design/hiring lead into someone who reads a full case study and comes
away convinced. It is built as a single continuous "altitude journey" — rising
into an expressive creative side (space), landing on who he is now (sky),
descending through the measured, shipped work (ground) — so the craft of the
site is itself the argument. Success looks like an evaluator who finishes a case
study (checkout, fair-pricing) and remembers the work afterward.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, expressive, immersive — grounded in measured rigor. The voice is
confident and specific, numbers-literate without bragging, warm rather than
clinical. Two registers are deliberately held together rather than averaged: the
measured side (ruled rows, tabular numerals, conversion outcomes) and the
expressive side (off-grid, suspended, 0→1 experiments). Emotional goals, in
order: awe at the craft, trust in the rigor, curiosity to read on.

## Anti-references

- **Templated portfolios** — generic Framer/Webflow template looks,
  dribbble-clone card grids, "available for work" badge clichés.
- **Corporate SaaS dashboard** — cold, gridded, enterprise-flavored; feels like
  a tool, not a person.
- **Sparse say-nothing minimalism** — so stripped-back it has no point of view;
  whitespace as a substitute for substance.
- **Loud over-animated agency site** — motion for motion's sake, scroll-jacking,
  effects that obstruct reading the work.

The line between the immersive intent and these traps: atmosphere and motion
exist only to pull a reader deeper into the work, never to perform or to block.

## Design Principles

1. **Outcomes over applause.** Every expressive choice ultimately points at a
   shipped result or a moved number. Beauty earns its place by serving the
   argument, not decorating it.
2. **The medium is the proof.** The site's own craft must demonstrate the skill
   being sold — no gap between the claim and the artifact in front of the
   evaluator.
3. **Immersion in service of reading.** Cinematic atmosphere and motion are tools
   to draw a time-poor lead further down toward a case study; the moment they
   slow reading, they've failed.
4. **Two registers, one person.** The measured (work/conversion) and the
   expressive (creative/space) are intentionally contrasted and held together,
   never blended into a safe middle.
5. **Earn the scroll.** Each zone must give a skeptical evaluator a concrete
   reason to continue, and the journey is composed to deposit them at a case
   study.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG conformance target. Hold to sensible defaults: maintain the
`prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives already built with `gsap.matchMedia`
(everything resolves to a static, readable state), keep the experience
keyboard-navigable, and keep body text comfortably readable against the dark,
moody palette (don't let "atmosphere" push contrast below legibility).
